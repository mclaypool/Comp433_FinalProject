We go Longer

NILE books dates back to our flagship store in Rio de Janerio, Brazil. Founded in 1911 as simply "NILE," the store sold general goods and had a special and extensive book and magazine collection.

The founder of Nile, Paloma Nickleso, was a lover and collector of texts from across the globe. Her passion for reading and knowledge was evident in the fast-growing reading section of NILE. Customers demanded more and more books, magazines, and articles.

By 1935, Paloma ceased selling general goods and launched two more locations of NILE in Rio. By 1945, NILE was rebranded as NILE books and spread to 13 citites and 45 locations in Brazil.

1962 was a big year for NILE: Paloma retired as CEO, promoted her most trusted employees to leadership, and opened 7 international locations in Paris, Seattle, New York, Beijing, Cairo, Sydney, and Manila.

Since 1995, when NILE first launched our online store, we have increased our sales from $1 billion annually. to over $13 billion annually. Our growth can be attributed to our dedication to listening to our customers.
